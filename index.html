The philosophy

Beyond the score

A single test score can't explain why one learner thrives and another quietly disengages. Get2KnoUrLearners looks at three more dimensions most assessment tools never touch — measured continuously, not just at the end.

Cognitive Engagement

How a learner actually perceives and connects with what they're being taught — not just whether they can recite it back.

  • Content perception: is the material coherent, plausible, credible, comprehensible?
  • Individual factors: attention, personal relevance, cultural orientation

Situational Motivation

Why a learner engages with the material — tracked module by module across the whole course, not assumed to stay constant.

  • Intrinsic motivation
  • Identified regulation
  • External regulation
  • Amotivation

Situational Cognitive Engagement

How a learner engages with the work itself, moment to moment, across every task and project in the course.

  • Engagement with the task at hand
  • Effort and persistence
  • Experience of flow

The science

Built on validated research, not guesswork

Assessment frameworks are never neutral — they embody a particular view of what learning is and how it should be evidenced. The three dimensions introduced above aren't measured by intuition — each maps to a specific, peer-reviewed, validated instrument:

Conceptual engagement

Measures whether learners find instructional content coherent, credible, plausible, and personally relevant — the conditions research shows are necessary for deep engagement with new ideas.

Heddy et al., 2018

Situational engagement

Captures engagement, effort, and flow as they actually occur within a specific activity — not as a vague retrospective impression.

Rotgans & Schmidt, 2011

Situational motivation

Grounded in Self-Determination Theory, distinguishing intrinsic interest from external pressure — because why a learner engages matters as much as whether they do.

Guay et al., 2000

Rigor under the hood. Every composite score is built on the same psychometric standards a peer-reviewed study would use — construct reliability, Item Response Theory for item-level quality, and transparent, versioned scoring — so results hold up to scrutiny, not just intuition.

Every instrument is fully swappable — bring your own validated scale, or use ours.

For researchers & partners

Where this came from

Get2KnoUrLearners started as a solution to a real research problem: existing survey and learning platforms couldn't support the sequential, module-linked, mixed-methods data collection a rigorous longitudinal study required. Rather than force a study design to fit an off-the-shelf tool, the platform was built from the ground up around what evidence-based learning research actually needs.

That origin matters. It means every design decision — the instrument choices, the scoring transparency, the audit trail — was made under the same standards a peer-reviewed study is held to, not retrofitted after the fact for a product pitch.

The longer vision

The goal isn't a one-time assessment tool — it's a sustainable ecosystem for ongoing, high-integrity education research. Used longitudinally, the platform can help build a per-learner picture that evolves over time: not just what a student knows today, but how their interests, engagement, and motivation are developing. Democratizing this kind of evaluation, so any educator or program leader can access research-grade insight without a research team, is the underlying goal.
