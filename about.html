About the researcher & developer

Soumya K. De

AI Literacy Researcher, University at Buffalo — Dept. of Learning & Instruction

Translating AI systems into cognitively grounded learning — with 20+ years building and deploying real-world technical systems before turning that experience toward education research.

Get2KnoUrLearners is built by a researcher who spent two decades as a systems architect and entrepreneur before turning to education research — not the usual path into edtech, and that's precisely what shaped the platform.

Soumya spent over 20 years across Silicon Valley and India in wireless systems engineering, technical marketing, and enterprise-scale systems integration — including roles at Cisco Systems and Symbol Technologies (now Motorola), followed by 17 years founding and leading two technology companies delivering IP/IoT automation, industrial monitoring, and energy systems for clients including Tata Motors and the National Council of Science Museums (Government of India). That work meant taking a concept all the way from grant proposal to a deployed, field-tested system used by real operators under real constraints — not a lab demo.

Since 2022, that same execution discipline has been applied to a different problem: evidence-based AI-literacy education for 10–14 year olds. As a PhD researcher in the University at Buffalo's Department of Learning & Instruction, Soumya designed, piloted, and evaluated the curriculum now running in libraries, community centers, and youth programs across Western New York — and, when no existing platform could support the mixed-methods data collection the research required, built one.

Why this background

The skills behind the platform

Get2KnoUrLearners required conceptualizing a research-grade assessment framework, grounding it in real learning-sciences theory, and then actually building and shipping working software — not just one of those things.

Systems & solutions architecture

Two decades designing and deploying multi-layer technical systems for enterprise and government clients translated directly into the platform's own four-layer architecture: student-facing data capture, a hybrid Qual+Quant analytics engine, a teacher-facing dashboard, and an administrative governance layer.

Applied statistics & assessment design

An Advanced Certificate in Applied Statistical Analysis, combined with hands-on R-based statistical modeling, underlies the platform's automatic inferential statistics and the same psychometric rigor — construct reliability, Item Response Theory — a peer-reviewed study would require.

AI evaluation & hands-on implementation

Python-LLM integration and rapid prototyping in Streamlit aren't outsourced skills — the platform's self-hosted, multi-model AI pipeline for qualitative thematic analysis was built and is maintained directly, with the same LLM orchestration and human-AI interaction expertise applied throughout.

Learning sciences & curriculum design

PhD coursework in Learning & Instruction grounds the platform's assessment philosophy in established theory — the same theoretical foundations (conceptual change, self-determination, situated cognition) cited throughout this site, not applied after the fact.

Founder-level execution

Seventeen years running technology companies means this platform was designed to actually ship — piloted with real students, real classrooms, and real data, on a path to a stable, institution-ready release, not left as an academic prototype.

Translating complexity for non-technical users

Years in technical marketing and pre-sales engineering — making complex wireless architectures understandable to enterprise customers — are exactly why this platform is built so a teacher with no statistics background or coding experience can use it confidently.
